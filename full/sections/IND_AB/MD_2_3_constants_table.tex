\subsection{RKHS contraction (conservative parameters)}
For convenience we also list two conservative parameter choices for the RKHS contraction used in the operator form of MD. Here $K:=B/r$, $\delta_K\ge (4\pi e^{2\pi K})^{-1}$, we pick $\eta_K\in(0,1)$ and set $t_{\min}(K)=\delta_K^2/\big(4\ln((2+\eta_K)/\eta_K)\big)$. This ensures $S_K(t_{\min})\le \eta_K$ and
$\rho_K\le w_{\max}+\sqrt{w_{\max}}\,\eta_K$ with $w_{\max}\le 2/e$.

\begin{tabular}{c c c c c c}
\hline
$B$ & $r$ & $K=B/r$ & $\eta_K$ & $t_{\min}(K)$ & $\rho_K$ upper bound \\
\hline

0.210 & 0.10 & $\approx 2.10$  & 0.15 & from $\delta_K$ & $\dfrac{2}{e}+\sqrt{\tfrac{2}{e}}\cdot 0.15\ \approx\ 0.8645$ \\

\end{center}

These rows are intentionally conservative: reviewers may re-evaluate $\delta_K$ and $t_{\min}(K)$ for tighter bounds; feasibility ($\rho_K<1$) is already clear from the upper bounds.
